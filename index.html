Our Purpose
We are grateful to the heroes that answer the call to serve and take an oath to defend and protect our freedom. How can the rest of us serve those who put our country first? By taking care of what's most precious to them, their children. There are 2 million children with a parent serving in the military. Too often military kids feel these burdens alone, putting them at risk for a range of emotional and behavioral problems, as well as poor academic outcomes. Our vision is to ensure all military children receive the support they need to thrive.

Our Challenge
We found that a parent deploying often leads to anger, confusion, and sadness. Military children are often asked to make this sacrifice, and the effects of uncertainty, fear, and loss at such a young age can be crippling. Imagine the fear of a parent leaving for what seems to be forever - sometimes never returning. Parents that return suffer physically, emotionally, or mentally. As a child, this is impossible to comprehend. Their lives can be isolating, leading to academic struggle, emotional limitations, and substance abuse. The children of our heroes should not be overlooked. We want to provide them with the tools and strategies to properly process their experiences.
